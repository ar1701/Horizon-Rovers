OUR ROVERS

MARS ROVER

2024

Mechanical Design

The Horizon Mars Rover is engineered for robust mobility across challenging terrains. A six-wheel rocker-bogie suspension system, coupled with high-torque motors, enables the rover to navigate obstacles up to 25 cm and slopes up to 40 degrees. The chassis, a sturdy platform housing electronics and mounting points, supports the robotic arm equipped with 6 DOF manipulators for versatile operations.

Electric Subsystem

Powered by a reliable 12V Li-ion battery pack, the rover prioritizes safety with features like fuses, isolators, and an emergency stop button. Continuous system monitoring ensures optimal performance and fault detection. Robust connectors and clear indicator lights enhance reliability and operational visibility.

Software and Navigation

Built on the ROS1 Noetic platform, the rover's software architecture facilitates communication and integration with various components. While primarily designed for manual operation, the rover incorporates basic autonomous capabilities including localization using AMCL and path planning with move_base.

Scientific Payload

The rover's probing unit, equipped with image processing capabilities, analyzes AR tags and QR codes. The robotic arm, in conjunction with ROS MoveIt and OpenCV, executes precise tasks such as probe handling and scientific experiments. A science unit, incorporating thermal imaging and image classification, contributes to comprehensive data collection and analysis.

Sensor Suite

A combination of accelerometer, gyroscope, and laser-ranging sensor provides essential data for localization, navigation, and obstacle avoidance. This sensor suite ensures the rover's ability to traverse complex Martian terrains effectively.

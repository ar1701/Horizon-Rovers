OUR ROVERS

TIRANGA

2024

Mechanical Design

In its third iteration, TIRANGA has been meticulously engineered for the European Rover Challenge (ERC) 2024, embodying a commitment to simplicity, efficiency, and a sleek, streamlined design. This 4-wheeled rover features a chassis constructed from aluminum using CNC machining and 3D printing techniques, resulting in a lightweight and durable structure. The design focuses on organic aesthetics without compromising functionality, ensuring no unnecessary bulk.

Robotic Arm and Tools

TIRANGA is equipped with a 5DOF robotic arm featuring a multipurpose gripper designed for critical maintenance tasks such as pressing buttons, toggling switches, and inserting plugs. Additionally, the arm excels in scientific operations like soil sampling and rock analysis. The inclusion of a drilling unit inspired by the Archimedes screw extends the rover's capabilities, allowing it to meet the diverse and demanding challenges of the ERC.

Software and Navigation

In this iteration, we transitioned to ROS2 Humble, significantly optimizing the rover’s software infrastructure and enhancing both autonomy and performance. We implemented USB serial communication for efficient and reliable interfacing with the rover's lower-level systems. The ZED2 depth camera, paired with advanced sensor fusion through an extended Kalman filter, provides precise localization and environmental awareness. For obstacle detection, we developed a custom model to identify traversal paths, working in tandem with the Hybrid A* path planning algorithm to ensure accurate and efficient route mapping.

Advanced Capabilities

The successful implementation of inverse kinematics for the robotic arm further augments TIRANGA’s capabilities, enabling it to perform intricate tasks with precision. This iteration emphasizes simplicity and efficiency, ensuring the rover is both lightweight and highly functional. The fully autonomous TIRANGA is designed to navigate complex terrains and perform a variety of tasks with minimal human intervention, showcasing advanced autonomy and robust performance.

Design Philosophy

TIRANGA’s design philosophy is rooted in organic, minimalistic aesthetics that do not compromise on functionality. This iteration features a sleek design aimed at reducing weight and improving efficiency. The combination of advanced mechanical, electrical, and software systems positions TIRANGA as a formidable contender in the ERC 2024, pushing the boundaries of planetary exploration and setting new standards in the field of autonomous robotics.

OUR ROVERS

PHOENIX

2024

Mechanical Design

The 2023 Phoenix iteration of our Mars rover project introduced a 6-wheeled design with significant weight reduction achieved through the use of primarily aluminum and custom components. The integration of a cycloidal gearbox with a differential wrist unit and a custom wheel fork improved the rover's efficiency and maneuverability. The 6 DOF robotic arm, driven by a worm gear for steering, was fabricated using advanced techniques such as 3D printing and sheet metal cutting and bending.

Electrical Systems

In this iteration, we continued to focus on robust electrical systems. The rover was powered by a reliable 12V Li-ion battery pack and equipped with safety features like fuses, isolators, and an emergency stop button. System monitoring was enhanced to ensure optimal performance and fault detection. Robust connectors and indicator lights were used to maintain operational visibility and reliability.

Software and Navigation

Phoenix utilized both ROS2 Foxy and ROS1 Noetic, connected through a serial bridge. This setup was necessary as our hardware was compatible only with ROS1, while various other components required ROS2. For perception, we used an Intel RealSense depth camera, and for obstacle detection, we implemented a YOLOv8 model. We also developed forward kinematics for the robotic arm. Despite these advancements, the system had not yet achieved full autonomy.

Scientific Payload

The rover was equipped with a telescopic drill designed for handling tasks related to navigation, science, and maintenance during the ERC 2023 mission. The scientific payload included instruments for conducting detailed observations and experiments, enabling the rover to contribute valuable data during the mission.

Fabrication and Customization

The introduction of custom components like the cycloidal gearbox and differential wrist unit, as well as the custom wheel fork, showcased our ability to innovate and tailor the rover to specific mission requirements. The use of 3D printing and sheet metal cutting and bending techniques allowed for precise fabrication and significant weight reduction, enhancing the rover's performance and durability in the challenging Martian environment.
